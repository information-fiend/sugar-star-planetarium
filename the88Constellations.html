The 88 Constellations

The 88 constellations officially recognized by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) cover the entire night sky and serve as a standardized map for astronomers. These constellations, which include both ancient and more recent groupings, help organize the sky into specific areas for easier identification and study of celestial objects. Each constellation has defined boundaries, allowing astronomers to precisely locate stars and other astronomical phenomena.
